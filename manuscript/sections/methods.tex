\subsection{Problem statement}

Given a state $S$ partitioned into atomic units $U = \{u_1, \ldots, u_M\}$
(census blocks, block groups, or VTDs) and a target district count $N$
fixed by apportionment, we seek a deterministic assignment
$\pi : U \to \{1, \ldots, N\}$ such that each district
$D_i = \pi^{-1}(i)$ is contiguous, non-empty, and close to representing
both $1/N$ of the people and $1/N$ of the geography of $S$.

Let $P = \sum_u \mathrm{pop}(u)$ and $A = \sum_u \mathrm{area}(u)$ denote
state population and area, with per-district targets $P^* = P/N$ and
$A^* = A/N$.

\subsection{Capacitated assignment}

The current implementation (the ``Population-Capacitated Voronoi
Baseline,'' v0) treats districting as a capacitated transportation
problem in the lineage of Hess et al.~\cite{hess1965}. Seeds
$\{s_1, \ldots, s_N\}$ are placed deterministically by farthest-point
sampling. In each Lloyd iteration~\cite{aurenhammer1987,cohenaddad2018},
$(u, i)$ pairs are sorted by normalized Euclidean distance
$\|c(u) - s_i\| / \mathrm{diam}(S)$ and assigned greedily, subject to the
hard capacity $\sum_{u \in D_i} \mathrm{pop}(u) \leq P^*$. Seeds are then
recomputed as population-weighted centroids and the assignment is
repeated until it stabilizes. Population balance is thus enforced as a
hard constraint at the assignment step, in keeping with
\textit{Reynolds v.~Sims}~\cite{reynolds1964}; area balance is reported
but not enforced.

Ties on minimum normalized distance break first by ascending unit ID,
then by ascending district ID, so the entire procedure is deterministic
and reproducible.

\subsection{DualBalance score}

The scoring harness reports two scalar measures of how balanced a plan
is on both dimensions. Define the per-district relative deviations
\[
  \mathrm{pop\_dev}_i  = \frac{\bigl|\mathrm{pop}(D_i)  - P^*\bigr|}{P^*},
  \qquad
  \mathrm{area\_dev}_i = \frac{\bigl|\mathrm{area}(D_i) - A^*\bigr|}{A^*},
\]
and let $\overline{\mathrm{pop\_dev}}$ and
$\overline{\mathrm{area\_dev}}$ denote their means over $i = 1, \ldots, N$.

The default \emph{weighted} score is the convex-combination form
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{DBS}_{\mathrm{w}}
    = \frac{1}{1 + \tfrac{1}{2}\,\overline{\mathrm{pop\_dev}}
                       + \tfrac{1}{2}\,\overline{\mathrm{area\_dev}}},
  \label{eq:dbs-weighted}
\end{equation}
weighting population and area equally so that each district is judged
on representing roughly $1/N$ of the people and $1/N$ of the state's
geography. The $0.5/0.5$ coefficients make the error a convex
combination rather than a sum, so adding the area term cannot inflate
the error beyond what either component carries on its own.

Alongside the weighted form, the harness also reports the
\emph{classic} sum-of-means score
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{DBS}_{\mathrm{c}}
    = \frac{1}{1 + \overline{\mathrm{pop\_dev}} + \overline{\mathrm{area\_dev}}}.
  \label{eq:dbs-classic}
\end{equation}
Both scores reach $1.0$ for a perfectly balanced plan and approach $0$
in the limit of unbounded deviation. The classic form is strictly more
punishing whenever either deviation is nonzero: at
$\overline{\mathrm{pop\_dev}} = \overline{\mathrm{area\_dev}} = 1.0$ the
weighted score is $0.5$, the classic score is $1/3$. The two are
related by $\mathrm{DBS}_{\mathrm{c}} = 1 / (1 + 2(1 - 1/\mathrm{DBS}_{\mathrm{w}}))$.

\subsection{Reynolds-tightening pass}

The capacity-greedy initial plan typically satisfies Reynolds tolerance
out of the box, but a population-tightening pass and an optional
pop-neutral area-reduction pass are available for plans that do not.
Phase A moves boundary units from over-target to adjacent under-target
districts until every district lies within a user-specified
$|\mathrm{pop}(D_i) - P^*|/P^* \leq \tau$ band (default $\tau = 0.005$).
A run-time flag selects the objective: \emph{weighted} (default) picks
the move that maximally reduces $\max_i |\mathrm{pop}(D_i) - P^*|$,
guaranteeing monotone $L^\infty$ improvement; \emph{classic} picks the
move that maximally reduces
$\overline{\mathrm{pop\_dev}} + \overline{\mathrm{area\_dev}}$, the
denominator of~(\ref{eq:dbs-classic}). Phase B then performs pairwise
swaps of equal-population boundary units that reduce area deviation
while keeping both districts within $\tau$.

\subsection{Out-of-scope inputs}

The generator reads only geography and population. Politics, race,
demographics, communities of interest, and competitiveness are not
inputs; partisan diagnostics may be \emph{reported} by the scoring
harness but are never fed back into the generator.
